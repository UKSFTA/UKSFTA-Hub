# [RESTRICTED] SAS TRAINING CURRICULUM

This document outlines the formalized syllabus for the Special Air Service (SAS) qualification course. Training is delivered in **2-hour modular sessions**.

---

## 1. PHASE 1: SELECTION & APTITUDE
**Objective:** Evaluation of physical endurance, navigational proficiency, and mental resilience.

1. **Aptitude Phase:** 2-hour high-intensity navigational march (Condensed Fan Dance).
2. **Navigational Assessment:** 2-hour solo point-to-point navigation module.
3. **Endurance Phase:** Modular 2-hour field exercise focusing on sustained operations under pressure.

---

## 2. PHASE 2: CONTINUATION TRAINING
**Objective:** Mastery of the fundamental tactical and technical skills of the SAS Trooper.

### 2.1 TACTICAL PATROL SKILLS (2-HOUR MODULE)
- **Contact Drills:** Standardised responses to hostile contact (Front, Rear, Flanks).
- **SERE:** Condensed Survival, Evasion, Resistance, and Extraction protocols.
- **Patrol SOPs:** Covert movement and the establishment of Patrol Harbors.

### 2.2 WEAPONRY & DEMOLITIONS (2-HOUR MODULE)
- **Platform Mastery:** Proficiency with unit-specific weapon systems (e.g., L119A2).
- **Demolitions:** Combat breaching and sabotage techniques using ACE-3 explosives.

### 2.3 ADVANCED COMMUNICATIONS & MEDICAL (2-HOUR MODULE)
- **ACRE-2:** Mastery of high-frequency (HF) and tactical radio systems.
- **Combat Medical:** Qualification as a Combat Medical Technician (CMT).

---

## 3. PHASE 3: TROOP SPECIALIZATION (2-HOUR MODULES)
Candidates are assigned to a specialist troop based on operational aptitude:
- **Air Troop:** HALO/HAHO parachuting and high-altitude operations.
- **Boat Troop:** Diving, surface swimming, and maritime infiltration.
- **Mountain Troop:** High-angle climbing and cold-weather operations.
- **Mobility Troop:** Vehicle-mounted tactics and heavy weapons employment.

---

## 4. QUALIFICATION
Candidates who demonstrate proficiency across all 2-hour modules are awarded the SAS qualification. Training may span multiple weeks to ensure all standards are met.
